Brain

The body's control center â€” thought, memory, and motor control.

The brain has major regions such as the cortex, cerebellum and brainstem. Traumatic injury, stroke, or uncontrolled blood pressure can cause serious damage. Seek emergency care for sudden weakness, facial droop, or speech difficulty.

Heart

The pump that circulates blood â€” essential for life.

Major causes of heart disease include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, poor diet and stress. Chest pain, sweating, nausea, or shortness of breath can indicate a heart attack â€” seek immediate care.

Lungs

Organs for oxygen intake and carbon dioxide removal.

Pollution, smoking and infections can harm the lungs. Persistent cough or shortness of breath should be evaluated by a physician.

Liver

Processes nutrients, detoxifies the body and supports metabolism.

Excess alcohol, medication misuse and viral infections can damage the liver. Symptoms may include loss of appetite, abdominal pain and jaundice.

Kidneys

Filter waste and excess fluid from the blood to make urine.

Diabetes and high blood pressure can damage the kidneys. Back pain, swelling in the legs or changes in urination should prompt testing.

Stomach

Starts digestion and helps break down food.

Spicy or contaminated food, infections and allergies can cause stomach problems. Seek care for severe pain, persistent vomiting or bloody stools.

Bones & Muscles

The body's framework and drivers of movement.

Vitamin D and calcium are essential for bone health. Injuries can cause fractures â€” get X-rays and care promptly.

Skin

Protects the body, regulates temperature, and senses touch.

Keep skin clean, use sunscreen and watch for signs of infection. See a dermatologist for persistent rashes or wounds.

Eyes

Organs of vision.

Redness, blurry vision or sudden loss of sight should be evaluated by an ophthalmologist. Regular check-ups help maintain eye health.

Ears

Responsible for hearing and balance.

Ear pain, discharge or hearing loss warrants ENT evaluation. Ear infections are common in children.

Reproductive System

Related to reproduction and hormonal health.

Consult specialists for pregnancy, fertility or menstrual concerns. Practice safe sexual health and seek guidance when needed.
